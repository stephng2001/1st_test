Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco. Iowa
  • 1914 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration".
  • 1914 - Has to stop school and save up money. Works in the Civilian Conservative Corps, helping starving Americans, "I saw how food chnaged them", he said, "All of this left scars on
    me."
  • 1914 - Finishes university and takes a job with the US Forestry Service.
  • 1914 - Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off duty due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, he returns to school study under Staman, who teaches him about
    breeding pest-resislent plant.
  • 1914 - Tries to enrol in the military after the Oearl Harbor attack, but is rejected. Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and
    other applied science.
  • 1914 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology.
  • 1914 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupent, leaves behind his pregnant wife, flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years, his team breeds
    6000 different strains of desease reistent wheat - including different varieties of each major climate change.
  • 1914 - Discovers a way to grow wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields.
  • 1914 - Crosses a short, study dwarf breed of wheat with a high-yielding American breed, creatind a strain that respond well to fertilizer. it goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat.

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution the one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace
progress"
